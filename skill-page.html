As a Programmer

Learning is a process

To be honest, I don't have a very long history of coding.

I had some attempts at learning to program at high school, I would watch some YouTube videos and try writing some small programs. But I never really learned much. Heck, I didn't even know which language I should start with. I tried C and Java, but I stopped after 2 weeks. I had no motivation, nor any goal in programming. However, I still liked the thinking and problem solving when I tried it, so I selected Computer Science as my major. Then I got really good with Java, but I was limited to Java. Sometimes I got Stack Overflow, and I would write crazy recursive functions because I had no knowledge in memory management.

Better late than never, I realized that being a computer science is very different from other majors. Classes are important, but because of the rapid rate of improvement in technology, the more important are new information and knowledge that I need to learn by myself just like the other professional programmers. Knowing things are good for working on a project, but it is even better to be able to learn and adapt to new knowledge quickly.

I am not yet a can-do-it-all programmer, but by receiving a large amount of information daily, I am in the process of becoming one.
